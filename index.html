Our Story & Mission

Serving Madhya Pradesh Since 2008

Amiti Welfare Society is a not‑for‑profit organisation working to build a healthy society for all, along with community‑based livelihood opportunities. Our work is primarily based in Bhopal, Sehore and surrounding regions of Madhya Pradesh, with programmes that combine healthcare access, health awareness, nutrition, mental health, and women’s economic empowerment.

We work hand-in-hand with municipal and state agencies, public systems, local institutions, CSR partners, and civil society to offer integrated, grassroots solutions. Our designs and implementations align directly with the Sustainable Development Goals, particularly SDG 3 (Good Health and Well-being) and SDG 5 (Gender Equality).

A NOTE FROM OUR FOUNDER

Message from the Founder

When I started Amiti, I wasn't thinking about building an organisation. I simply wanted to help people.

Over time, I realised that some of the biggest health challenges in our communities are not caused by a lack of treatment, but by a lack of awareness. People often don't know when to seek help, what is normal, or where to find reliable information.

Amiti was created to bridge that gap. We believe that healthcare begins with knowledge, conversations and community support. By making preventive healthcare interactive, inclusive and accessible, we hope to empower people to make informed choices for themselves and their families.

This is only the beginning, and we look forward to creating healthier communities together.

Amit Kimtee Founder, Amiti Social Welfare Society

Mission

Our mission is to:

  • ✓ Strengthen community‑based, preventive and promotive healthcare systems with a special focus on women, adolescents and other underserved groups.
  • ✓ Empower women and girls with knowledge, agency and skills so they can actively shape decisions about their health, livelihoods and well‑being.
  • ✓ Build sustainable, community‑owned social enterprises and livelihood models, in convergence with National Rural Livelihoods Mission (NRLM) frameworks and women‑centric government schemes.
  • ✓ Collaborate with public systems, local institutions, CSR partners and civil society to offer integrated solutions around health, nutrition, mental health, safety and economic resilience, in line with the Ministry of Women and Child Development’s mandate for holistic development of women and children.

Our Core Goals

Preventive & Promotive Healthcare

  • ✓ Increase access to regular health screening, early identification and timely referral for women, adolescents, children and vulnerable groups.
  • ✓ Strengthen community awareness on nutrition, menstrual health, mental health, and non‑communicable diseases, aligned with SDG 3 targets.

Women’s Agency & Livelihoods

  • ✓ Organise and capacitate women (including SHG members) as health ambassadors, community mobilisers and micro‑entrepreneurs, in the spirit of DAY‑NRLM.
  • ✓ Build market‑linked, sustainable livelihood opportunities that enhance women’s income, decision‑making power and financial resilience.

Safe, Inclusive Spaces

  • ✓ Create and nurture safe physical and social spaces in communities where women, adolescents and vulnerable groups can access information, counselling, and care.
  • ✓ Integrate principles of dignity, non‑discrimination and gender‑sensitivity, complementing WCD initiatives.

Systems Convergence

  • ✓ Forge strong partnerships with government departments (Health, WCD, Rural Development/NRLM), local bodies, and corporates to scale interventions.
  • ✓ Align programme design and impact measurement with SDGs, national health priorities and women‑centric schemes.

Thematic Focus and Alignment

Preventive healthcare and health literacy

School‑ and community‑based camps, screenings and awareness on physical and mental health, hygiene, nutrition and lifestyle diseases.

Focus on early detection and counselling rather than only episodic curative care.

Women‑centred health and livelihoods

Programmes that place women at the centre as decision‑makers, care‑seekers and community resource persons, echoing NRLM’s focus on women‑led SHGs and enterprises.

Livelihood interventions (e.g., stitching and other skills) linked to dignified work, local demand and social impact.

Convergence with Government priorities

Alignment with SDG 3 and 5; National Health Mission priorities on maternal, adolescent and mental health; and WCD schemes on nutrition, safety and empowerment.

Scope for direct CSR‑supported pilots that can later be absorbed or scaled in partnership with government systems.

A path of consistent, trust-driven growth and commitment.

Inception & Foundation

Amiti Welfare Society was registered under the M.P. Societies Registration Act. We launched our first primary healthcare camp in Lal Ghati with local volunteer doctors.

Conducted our 50th health camp and set up diagnostic channels for rural districts, focusing heavily on primary checks for adolescent girls.

Seeing the nutrition challenges of cancer patients and caregivers, we launched the continuous daily meal drive at Cancer Hospital, Bhopal.

2020

Pandemic Response & Awareness

Shifted resources to COVID-19 relief, mask distribution, sanitation workshops, and facilitated vaccine drives with public health authorities.

2026

New Flagship Initiatives

Obtained MCA CSR Form-1. Design and expansion of The Health Lab and The MENSES Project across Bhopal and Sehore districts.

Featured Opportunity

Mental Health Internship Program

Gain Hands-on Experience in Community Mental Health Advocacy & Public Health

Program Overview

Our Mental Health Internship Program offers students and young professionals an opportunity to gain meaningful exposure to community mental healthcare through a blend of clinical observation, fieldwork, and public health initiatives.

What You'll Experience

  • Clinical Exposure: Observe and learn alongside experienced psychiatrists, gaining insight into mental health practice and patient care.
  • Field Experience: Participate in community outreach, awareness camps, and mental health education initiatives across schools, colleges, sports clubs, and community settings.
  • Work with the Amiti Team: Collaborate with our multidisciplinary team to design, facilitate, and evaluate mental health awareness programs.
  • Skill Development: Build practical skills in psychoeducation, community engagement, communication, program planning, and behaviour change interventions.
  • Real-World Learning: Understand how preventive mental healthcare can be delivered outside clinical settings through evidence-based, community-centred approaches.

Eligibility Details

  • Pursuing Undergraduate (UG) or Postgraduate (PG) degrees in Psychology, Applied Psychology, Clinical Psychology, Psychiatric Social Work, or Human Development.
  • Young professionals, practitioners, and counseling aspirants looking for field-level community mental health exposure.
  • Candidates with a strong interest in public health, community service, and mental health advocacy.
  • Typically requires a minimum commitment of 4-8 weeks.
Program Details
Duration Flexible options of 1-month or 2-month cohorts
Mode Hybrid (Fieldwork in Bhopal & Sehore + clinical observership + online mentoring)
Clinical Observership Weekly observation sessions with registered psychiatrists & therapists
Mentorship Dedicated feedback sessions supervised by qualified psychologists
Recognition Certificate of Completion & Letter of Recommendation (LOR)
